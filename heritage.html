KOLKATA HERITAGE SITES

Indian Museum

Asia's largest museum

It is located on Chowringhee Road (now J.L. Nehru Road) and houses a collection of Indian natural history that rivals the Smithsonian Institution and the British Museum. The Indian Museum has an outstanding historical library, with a special focus on the Raj and Kolkata

Victoria Memorial

Opened in 1921

The Victoria Memorial was constructed in the heart of the City of Joy to honor Queen Victoria, after her death. Lord Curzon's brainchild commissioned the Victoria Memorial, which was modeled after the Taj Mahal. In Kolkata, it is viewed with pride and excitement and is colloquially referred to as the "Victoria."

Dakshineswar Kali Temple

Goddess kali

Bhavatarini,is a kind of Kali,located on the eastern bank of the Hooghly River. The temple is well-known for its links to Ramakrishna and Ma Sarada Devi, two 19th-century Bengali mysticsKali , also known as Kalika , is a Hindu goddess.Kali's earliest appearance is that of a destroyer of evil forces

Town Hall

Built in 1813

Town Hall is one of Kolkata's most significant heritage structures. This monumental hall is historically significant due to its magnificent architecture. It was the site of some of the most important meetings and conferences during the British occupation, the images of which are shown on the walls in the form of portraits and other types
